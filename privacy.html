Privacy Policy

Effective Date: April 6, 2026

1. Who We Are

Helios Integrative Health ("Helios," "we," "us," or "our") provides integrative behavioral health services — including psychotherapy, coaching, hypnotherapy, and coordinated specialist care — primarily via telehealth. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and protect your personal information when you visit our website at heliosintegrativehealth.com, use the Helios Life Inventory assessment, or communicate with us through our contact forms.

2. Information We Collect

We may collect the following categories of personal information:

  • Contact information — your name, email address, and phone number, provided when you submit a consultation request
  • Care preferences — the focus area and modality preferences you select in our intake form
  • Assessment responses — information you provide when completing the Helios Life Inventory
  • Communications — any additional information you share in the free-text field of our contact form or in subsequent correspondence
  • Usage data — standard web analytics information such as pages visited, browser type, and referring URL, collected through cookies or similar technologies

We do not collect Social Security numbers, financial account information, or government-issued identification through our website.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the personal information we collect for the following purposes:

  • To respond to your consultation requests and schedule appointments
  • To provide, coordinate, and improve our integrative health services
  • To communicate with you about your care, including follow-up correspondence
  • To operate, maintain, and improve our website and assessment tools
  • To comply with applicable legal obligations

4. We Do Not Sell or Share Your Information

Helios does not sell, rent, or share your personal information with third parties for marketing, advertising, or any commercial purpose.

We will never sell your personal information as defined under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA). We do not share your personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising.

The only circumstance in which we disclose personal information to a third party is at your express request — specifically, when you ask us to coordinate care on your behalf with practitioners within our referral network (the "Orbital Network"). In that case, we share only the information necessary to facilitate your care, and only with the specific providers you have authorized.

5. Your California Privacy Rights

If you are a California resident, you have the following rights under the CCPA/CPRA:

  • Right to Know — You may request that we disclose the categories and specific pieces of personal information we have collected about you, the sources of that information, the purposes for collection, and any third parties with whom it has been shared.
  • Right to Delete — You may request that we delete the personal information we have collected from you, subject to certain legal exceptions.
  • Right to Correct — You may request that we correct inaccurate personal information we maintain about you.
  • Right to Opt-Out of Sale or Sharing — We do not sell or share your personal information. No opt-out action is required.
  • Right to Non-Discrimination — We will not discriminate against you for exercising any of your privacy rights.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the information provided in Section 10 below. We will respond to verifiable consumer requests within 45 days as required by law.

6. Health Information Notice

Our website contact forms and the Life Inventory assessment are not HIPAA-secure communication channels. Please do not submit sensitive medical records, diagnoses, or detailed health histories through these forms. Information submitted through our website is used solely to facilitate scheduling and initial consultation.

Once you become an active client, any protected health information (PHI) exchanged in the course of clinical treatment is governed by HIPAA and handled through our secure electronic health record system (Simple Practice), not through this website.

7. Cookies & Tracking

Our website may use cookies and similar technologies to improve your browsing experience and to collect aggregate usage data. We do not use cookies for targeted advertising or to build profiles for third-party marketing. You may adjust your browser settings to refuse cookies, though some features of the site may not function as intended.

8. Data Security

We implement reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect your personal information from unauthorized access, disclosure, alteration, and destruction. However, no method of electronic transmission or storage is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will revise the "Effective Date" at the top of this page. We encourage you to review this policy periodically. Continued use of our website after changes are posted constitutes your acceptance of the revised policy.
