Washington Licensure

Washington-Licensed, Washington-Aware

Francesca holds licensure as a Licensed Mental Health Counselor (LMHC) through the Washington State Department of Health — the governing body for mental health clinicians practicing in the state.

The LMHC credential in Washington requires a graduate-level degree in counseling, psychology, or a related field, extensive post-degree supervised clinical hours, and successful passage of the National Counselor Examination (NCE) or equivalent. It authorizes the practice of psychotherapy with individuals, couples, and groups across the full range of mental and behavioral health concerns.

Washington state has made meaningful investments in mental health access and telehealth infrastructure, and the LMHC credential is among the most established pathways for licensed clinical care. Your sessions are conducted by a clinician who meets Washington's standards, operates under ethical guidelines enforced by the DOH, and carries professional liability insurance.

Telehealth Location Requirement

Washington telehealth law requires clients to be physically located within Washington state at the time of each session. If you are traveling out of state, sessions must be paused until you return. Francesca is also licensed in California, so clients who split time between both states can continue uninterrupted.

Who Francesca Works With

Therapy That Understands Washington Life

Washington state carries a particular quality of life — proximity to wilderness, a strong culture of independence, and, for many, the relentless pace of the technology sector. Francesca works with clients who are navigating all of it.

From Seattle's tech-forward identity to Tacoma's working-class roots, from Spokane's quieter rhythms to Bellevue's suburb-city tension, Washington offers a diverse emotional landscape. Francesca works with clients across Seattle, Bellevue, Tacoma, Spokane, Everett, Olympia, Bellingham, Vancouver, and communities throughout the state — all via secure telehealth.

Common presenting concerns from Washington clients include burnout and identity questions in the tech industry, relationship difficulty that surfaces alongside career-building, grief and loss, seasonal depression amplified by long winters and limited daylight, anxiety and depression that co-exist with high functioning, and existential questions that arise in a culture that prizes productivity over reflection.

For couples, Washington's dual-income, achievement-oriented culture can quietly erode connection. Francesca's Imago Therapy training helps partners develop the communication depth to stay genuinely close — not just logistically synchronized.

Getting Started

Therapy, Without the Commute

Starting therapy in Washington through Helios requires a private space, a reliable internet connection, and the willingness to begin. Sessions take place via SimplePractice — a HIPAA-compliant telehealth platform accessible through any browser on laptop, tablet, or phone.

Your first step is a free 15-minute consultation call. Francesca will listen to what brings you in, explain her approach, and together you'll decide whether the fit is right. A first full session is typically available within one to two weeks of that call.

Insurance & Fees in Washington

Francesca is an out-of-network provider in Washington state. Clients with PPO plans through employer-sponsored or private insurance may be eligible for partial out-of-network reimbursement using a superbill provided after each session. Washington's mental health parity laws mean your insurer must evaluate mental health claims the same way they evaluate physical health claims. Fee details are discussed during the free consultation.
