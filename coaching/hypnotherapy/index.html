Hypnotherapy for Habits & Performance

The mind changes faster when you work with it at the level where habits are actually stored. Coaching hypnotherapy combines the precision of hypnotic focus with Nicholas Tucker's coaching frameworks to create lasting change in performance, patterns, and mindset — without the clinical context.

Scope & Positioning

Coaching Hypnotherapy Is Not Clinical Therapy

This is a non-clinical service. It is important to understand what that means before going further.

Coaching hypnotherapy at Helios is delivered by Nicholas Tucker in his capacity as an executive and performance coach. It is not psychotherapy. It does not diagnose or treat mental health conditions. It is not appropriate for trauma, clinical anxiety disorders, depression, phobias with significant functional impairment, or any other diagnosed mental health condition.

If your concern is clinical in nature, please start here instead:

Francesca Licciardi, LMHC, LPCC, offers clinical hypnotherapy as part of her licensed therapy practice — appropriate for trauma, anxiety disorders, phobias, depression, and medically-adjacent goals like pain management and smoking cessation. If you are unsure whether your concern is clinical, the clinical track is always the appropriate starting point.

Coaching hypnotherapy is the right fit when your goal is non-clinical: shifting a performance pattern, building a habit, working through a limiting belief that affects your professional life, or accessing deeper focus and creative states. If that's where you are, read on.

What It's For

Performance Patterns That Resist Willpower

Some goals don't yield to logic. You know exactly what you want to change. You've tried to change it. And something beneath the surface keeps pulling you back.

This is where hypnotherapy is particularly effective. Many performance patterns — procrastination, self-sabotage, imposter syndrome, the particular anxiety that shows up before high-stakes presentations — are not rational in origin. They are habit loops, often formed early, operating faster than conscious thought. Willpower works at the wrong level.

Coaching hypnotherapy addresses these patterns at the level where they live: in the habitual, associative, pre-verbal mind. By guiding focused attention and receptivity, it creates the conditions for new responses to become automatic — not through repetition alone, but through genuine neurological shift.

Focus & Concentration

Building the capacity for deep work — sustained attention, reduced distraction, and the ability to enter flow states with intention rather than luck.

Procrastination & Avoidance

Most procrastination is anxiety avoidance in disguise. Coaching hypnotherapy addresses the emotional root of avoidance patterns, not just the behavioral symptom.

Confidence & Presence

Building genuine confidence in specific high-stakes contexts: public speaking, board presentations, client negotiations, performance reviews, difficult conversations.

Limiting Beliefs

The internal narratives that cap performance — "I'm not a leader," "I don't deserve this," "I'll be found out" — often respond better to hypnotic reframing than to conscious argument.

Habit Formation

Building and sustaining habits that support high performance: sleep hygiene, exercise consistency, dietary patterns, morning routines, and recovery practices.

Creative Access

Reaching the generative, associative mind that produces insight — particularly useful for creative professionals, strategists, and anyone whose work requires original thinking under pressure.

The Approach

Hypnotherapy Inside the Coaching Container

Coaching hypnotherapy with Nicholas is not a standalone hypnosis session tacked onto a coaching call. It is an integrated part of his coaching practice — hypnotic work in service of clearly defined coaching goals.

Sessions begin with coaching conversation: clarifying the goal, surfacing what's present, identifying what's in the way. Nicholas then moves into the hypnotic portion — guiding you into a focused, receptive state using voice, pacing, and targeted language. In this state, the coaching work continues at a deeper register: visualizations, suggestion, and direct engagement with the pattern you're working to change.

Sessions are typically 60 to 75 minutes. Some clients integrate coaching hypnotherapy into an ongoing coaching engagement; others come for a focused series of 4 to 6 sessions targeting a specific concern. All sessions are conducted via secure telecoaching — accessible from anywhere.

Nicholas integrates hypnotherapy with Positive Intelligence (PQ) frameworks when working on Saboteur patterns, and with Co-Active tools when working on identity-level beliefs. The approaches are complementary and are calibrated to what each client needs.
